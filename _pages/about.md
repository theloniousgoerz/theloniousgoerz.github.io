I am a fourth-year Ph.D. candidate in the [Department of Sociology](https://sociology.cornell.edu/) and the [Brooks School of Public Policy](https://publicpolicy.cornell.edu/) at Cornell University. I use **demographic, causal inference, and computational methods** to study historical and contemporary topics in racial stratification, residential segregation and diversity, and health.

Currently I am working on two projects: the first focuses on the causal effects of segregation on Black–White longevity disparities; the second seeks to understand emergent patterns of skin tone stratification among white Americans. Previously, I was a pre-doctoral research fellow in sociology and economics at Johns Hopkins University, and I graduated from the University of Washington, Seattle with a B.A. in sociology.
